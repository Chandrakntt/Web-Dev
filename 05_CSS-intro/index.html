Indian Constitution


The Indian Constitution, adopted on January 26, 1950, is the supreme law of India and lays the foundation for the country's political framework, legal system, and governance. It is the longest written constitution in the world, reflecting the diversity and complexity of the nation. Drafted by the Constituent Assembly under the leadership of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, it establishes India as a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic. The Constitution guarantees fundamental rights to all citizens, promotes justice, liberty, and equality, and provides a federal structure with a strong central government.
