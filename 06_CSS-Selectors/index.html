BrahMos missile system

The BrahMos missile system is a supersonic cruise missile developed jointly by India and Russia, named after the Brahmaputra and Moskva rivers. Known for its high speed (Mach 2.8 to 3.0), precision, and versatility, it can be launched from land, sea, air, and even submarines, making it a key part of India's strategic arsenal. The missile has a range of around 300 to 500 kilometers, depending on the variant, and is capable of carrying conventional warheads. Its ability to fly at low altitudes and follow terrain-hugging trajectories makes it difficult to intercept, giving India a significant edge in modern warfare.

key features of the BrahMos missile system:

  1. Supersonic Speed: Travels at speeds of Mach 2.8 to 3.0, making it one of the fastest cruise missiles in the world.
  2. Multi-platform Launch Capability: Can be launched from land-based mobile launchers, ships, submarines, and aircraft (like the Su-30MKI).
  3. Range: Varies by variant — up to 500 km for newer versions (originally ~290 km to comply with MTCR limits before India’s entry into the regime).
  4. High Accuracy: Precision strike capability with a circular error probable (CEP) of less than 1 meter.
  5. Versatile Warhead Options: Can carry conventional warheads weighing up to 200–300 kg.
  6. Stealth Features: Designed with low radar signature and terrain-hugging capability, making detection and interception difficult.
  7. Fire-and-Forget System: Does not require further guidance after launch.
  8. Two-stage Propulsion: Uses a solid-fuel booster for initial acceleration and a liquid-fuel ramjet for sustained supersonic cruise.
  9. Quick Reaction Time: Due to high speed and advanced guidance systems.
  10. Indigenous Components: Increasing localization in production, including efforts toward a BrahMos-NG (Next Generation) variant that will be lighter and more compact.
